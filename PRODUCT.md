# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Nostalgia serves two closely related user contexts:

- Public readers visit the blog to read articles, search archives, browse categories, and participate in comments after logging in.
- The site owner uses the admin surface to write, edit, publish, organize, and maintain blog content without a separate enterprise admin product.

## Product Purpose

Nostalgia is a personal blog system that keeps public reading and private authoring in one coherent frontend. Success means the public site feels polished and comfortable to read, while `/backend` gives the owner a focused writing and content-management workspace that is fast to maintain.

## Brand Personality

Refined, archival, calm.

The interface should feel precise and personal: a curated technical archive rather than a generic dashboard. It can use glass-like surfaces where they serve depth and focus, but reading clarity and task flow take priority over decoration.

## Anti-references

- Geeker Admin style enterprise dashboards, dense RBAC menus, generic system-management screens, and Element Plus visual defaults.
- Heavy blue-gray glass themes that feel like default AI dashboard styling.
- Marketing landing-page composition inside the product surface: oversized heroes, decorative metric cards, and explanatory feature copy.
- UI libraries or component choices that create a second visual language beside the public blog.

## Design Principles

- Keep reading and writing visually continuous: authored CKEditor content should resemble the published article view.
- Treat admin as a focused owner tool, not a multi-tenant management console.
- Reuse the public frontend's design tokens, theme mode, icon vocabulary, and feedback patterns.
- Prefer restrained density: compact controls, clear hierarchy, stable layouts, and direct actions.
- Let glass surfaces support separation and depth; avoid using blur as decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for readable contrast, visible focus states, keyboard-accessible navigation and forms, persistent light/dark/system theme support, and reduced-motion-friendly interactions. Public article reading should remain comfortable on mobile and desktop.
